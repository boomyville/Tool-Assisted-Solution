# DIY Companion Wireframe Testing Questions

Use these prompts during user interviews to evaluate usability, clarity, trust, and flow quality across the wireframes.

## 30 UI/UX Questions for User Testing

1. When you first land on Home, what do you think this app helps you do?
2. Is "+ New Project" the first thing you would tap? If not, what would you tap instead?
3. Do "My Projects" vs "Completed" feel clearly different and easy to scan?
4. When you open "Completed," is the expand/collapse behavior obvious?
5. In Project Input, is it clear what level of detail to write in the project description?
6. Does the photo upload area feel optional or required, and is that what you expected?
7. Do the skill-level options describe you accurately, or do they feel too broad/narrow?
8. After choosing a skill level, do you feel confident the app understood your ability correctly?
9. On the Assessment screen, do "DIY Approved," difficulty, and estimated time feel trustworthy?
10. Does the "Not for me — find a professional" option feel visible enough when you need it?
11. In Plan Overview, can you quickly tell what is completed, what is next, and where to start?
12. Are stage rows and arrows clearly tappable, or do you expect other elements (like checkboxes) to be interactive?
13. Does the progress indicator (for example, 1/3) motivate you and set expectations appropriately?
14. In Parts & Tools, do link and image toggles make sense without explanation?
15. Would you use the notes area in each part card, and what note prompts would make it more useful?
16. In Stage Detail, do hazard warnings feel noticeable at the right moment without being overwhelming?
17. Is "Ask a question" in the stage flow easy to find when you are stuck?
18. In the chat screen, does the conversation context ("Re: Stage 3") make your question feel grounded enough?
19. If a safety flag appears, do the options help you decide what to do next with confidence?
20. At any point in the flow, did you feel lost about where you are, how to go back, or what action to take next?
21. Please create a new project for a simple home fix, then tell me how easy or hard that felt.
22. Please enter your project description and pick your skill level, then tell me how confident you feel that the app understood you.
23. Please review the assessment result and decide whether to continue or find a professional, then tell me how trustworthy that decision point feels.
24. Please open the project plan and choose what you would do first, then tell me whether the order of stages feels logical.
25. Please expand one stage from the plan list, then tell me how clear the expanded details are.
26. Please go to Parts and Tools and toggle links and images on and off, then tell me whether those controls feel useful or distracting.
27. Please open a stage detail screen and read the hazard section, then tell me how safe and informed you feel.
28. Please use the Ask a question option from stage detail and imagine sending a question with a photo, then tell me how supported you feel.
29. Please move to the chat screen and continue the conversation, then tell me whether the replies feel relevant to your exact problem.
30. Please return from chat to your stage and continue the task, then tell me whether switching contexts feels smooth or confusing.
